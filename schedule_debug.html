Open swim times vary daily

The UCalgary Aquatic Centre is open most days with many times reserved for specific user groups. Active Living members or visitors who purchase a drop-in pass are welcome to the following pool sessions: 

  • Adult/Youth Lane Swim
  • Family and Lane Swim
  • Inflatable Swim

University of Calgary Aquatic Centre pool

Wristbands are required

All pool users must check-in at Client Services to obtain a wristband before accessing the Aquatic Centre. Those who do not have a wristband will be asked to return to Client Services to obtain a wristband.

Adult/Youth Lane Swim

  • Times and assigned pool space are indicated on the weekly schedule.
    • Typically three sessions a day, Monday through Friday: a morning session, a lunch hour session and an evening session.
    • Days and times will vary based on user groups and special events.
  • This is a lane swim opportunity to practice your swimming skills with 25m or 50m lanes.
  • Pull buoys are available to use.
  • A swim test may be required in order to access the deep end.

Age Restriction

Lane swimmers must be 12 years or older and participating in lane swim only.

Slow, Medium, Fast

Please select the most appropriate lane based on your swimming ability.

Circle Swim

Swimmers must swim in circular patterns, as posted on the lane signs. 

Family and Lane Swimming

  • Times and assigned pool space are indicated on the weekly schedule
    • Typically one session a day on Saturdays and Sundays
    • Days and times will vary, based on user groups and special events
  • Defined family swim space available in both the shallow and the deep end
  • 25m defined lanes for lane swimming in the shallow end
  • Floating mats, foam logs and preschool slide are available
  • Flutter boards and pull buoys are freely available
  • Swim test may be required in order to access the deep end

Stay Alert

Children under 7 must be within arms reach of a parent/guardian at all times.

Adult-Child Ratios

One parent or guardian must be present for every two children 0-7 years old.

Slow, Medium, Fast

Lane swimmers: please select the most appropriate lane based on swimming ability.

Circle Swim

Swimmers must swim in circular patterns, as posted on lane signs.

Inflatable Swim

  • Not regularly offered to ensure safety and overall logistics of the pool
  • A series of large inflatable toys are linked together, creating a floating obstacle course
  • The pool is set up in a 50m format

Age Restriction

Children must be at least 5 years old for inflatable swim.

Test Required

Swim test is required to access the inflatables.

Adult-Child Ratios

Parents supervision is required for children 5-7 using the inflatable.

Please Remember

  • No diving
  • No lane swim
  • No family swim

The University of Calgary, located in the heart of Southern Alberta, both acknowledges and pays tribute to the traditional territories of the peoples of Treaty 7, which include the Blackfoot Confederacy (comprised of the Siksika, the Piikani, and the Kainai First Nations), the Tsuut’ina First Nation, and the Stoney Nakoda (including Chiniki, Bearspaw, and Goodstoney First Nations). The city of Calgary is also home to the Métis Nation within Alberta (including Nose Hill Métis District 5 and Elbow Métis District 6).

The University of Calgary is situated on land Northwest of where the Bow River meets the Elbow River, a site traditionally known as Moh’kins’tsis to the Blackfoot, Wîchîspa to the Stoney Nakoda, and Guts’ists’i to the Tsuut’ina. On this land and in this place we strive to learn together, walk together, and grow together “in a good way.”
